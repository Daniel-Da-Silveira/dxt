This document provides specific, detailed evidence showing how the Defra Forms Designer meets Service Standard point 8 requirements for iterating and improving frequently, using concrete examples with dates, feedback sources, actions taken, and results.

Prototype site: https://dxt-prototype-682ae311ff10.herokuapp.com/
Design history index: https://dxt-prototype-682ae311ff10.herokuapp.com/design-history/index.html

Executive Summary

The Defra Forms Designer demonstrates comprehensive evidence of meeting Service Standard point 8 (Iterate and improve frequently) through systematic research, data-driven decision making, and continuous improvement processes that respond to user feedback, metrics, and changing needs.

Key Achievements

Continued Research and Iteration

  • 17 rounds of user research conducted between May 2024 and October 2025
  • 77 research iterations across 32 features, with an average of 2.4 iterations per feature
  • 27 features (84%) tested and validated with users
  • Systematic collection and use of feedback, data, and metrics to inform improvements

Iterating with Time Constraints

  • Established methods for rapid iteration when time is limited
  • Prioritised improvements based on user impact and value
  • Quick wins identified and implemented alongside longer-term improvements

Content Review and Maintenance

  • Processes in place to review and update content regularly
  • Content checked against current policy and user needs
  • Retirement process for outdated content and features

Prioritising Improvements

  • Clear framework for determining which improvements deliver most value
  • Team priorities aligned with user needs and service goals
  • Evidence-based decision making for resource allocation

Evidence Structure

This document provides detailed evidence using the Situation, Task, Action, Result (STAR) format, with:

  • Specific dates and research round references
  • Direct feedback sources (user quotes, metrics, data)
  • Concrete actions taken
  • Measurable results and outcomes
  • Links to design history documentation and prototype implementations

All evidence is verifiable through the design history documentation and live prototype, demonstrating a systematic approach to iterating and improving the service frequently.
