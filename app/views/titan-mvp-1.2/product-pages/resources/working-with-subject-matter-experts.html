There could be lots of people involved in a form.

Start by figuring out:

  • who has information you need to make the form
  • who will help draft
  • who needs to sign off

Screening questions

Use these screening questions to check if your SME is prepared to make a form.

  • What's the name and email address of the form owner?
  • If the form exists, is there a link to it?
  • If the form is updated frequently?
  • How many form submissions do you expect per year? (an estimate is fine but try and find evidence if you can)
  • What is the email address submissions will go to?
  • Do you need to collect payment from users?
  • Do you need location data for premises that don't have an address?
  • Do you need to collect documents and images?
  • Do users need to answer a set of repeated questions?
  • Will the form need to calculate answers provided by the user?
  • Is there an existing guide on GOV.UK?
  • Do you have a deadline and what's the reason for it?
  • How long does your form need to be available GOV.UK?

Exploration workshop

You might want to host a workshop with your SME to gather more requirements. You can use the Mural workshop template or the Defra Mural template. Update the board with any information you have and consider:

  • asking a second content designer to help you take notes
  • recording the exploration workshop to refer to later
  • using the auto transcript feature
