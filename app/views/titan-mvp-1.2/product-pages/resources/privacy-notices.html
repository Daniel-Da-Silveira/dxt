Most online forms need a privacy notice.

A privacy notice clearly explains:

  • step by step, what you'll do with the personal information once you've collected it
  • why you're collecting personal information
  • which of the legal bases you're using for collecting and processing personal information
  • how long you'll keep the personal information - or, if there's no set period, how you'll decide how long to keep it

Read the guidance on collection personal information from users and point 9 of the Service Standard.

See an example privacy notice for a form.

Check if your form is covered by an existing privacy notice

Ask your SME if they already have a privacy notice on GOV.UK for a related form. In some instances, you can use the standard privacy notice for each organisation. The Data Protection team for each organisation can advise on which privacy notice to use.

Data protection impact assessments

Ask your SME to fill in the data protection impact assessment questions - each Defra group organisation has their own screening question form.

Each Data Protection team will use the answers to the screening questions to determine if a full data protection impact assessment is needed. Most form requests do not need a full impact assessment.

Privacy notice templates

Templates are available if you need a new privacy notice.

Defra and APHA

Use the general privacy notice template from the privacy notice templates (collecting personal data) intranet page. Follow the guidance within the template to either add or remove information.

The SME will also need to fill in the Defra and APHA screening questions

Environment Agency (EA)

Ask the SME to review the Environment Agency data protection policies and guidance and fill in the DPIA screening form.

Use the EA template to draft the privacy notice.

You can also contact the EA Data Protection team email address on dataprotection@environment-agency.gov.uk

Data protection team contacts

Check the directory for a list of Defra organisations and who to contact about data protection and privacy notices.
