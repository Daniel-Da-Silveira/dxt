Each additional question in a form increases the risk that someone will abandon the form.

Question protocols help you to collect only the right information. But they take a while to do, so best used for long and complex forms.

Question protocols typically cover these themes and questions.

Usage

  • Why do we need this information?
  • What decisions are made with it?
  • Is it mandatory? If not, why are we still asking for it?
  • Where is it used? By whom?

Source

  • Do we already have this data?
  • How easy is it for the user to provide it?
  • When is it needed (for example, after an eligibility check)?
  • What format do we need it in?

Veracity

  • How will we validate the information is correct?
  • What happens if the data is wrong or missing?

Maintenance

  • How often is this information likely to change?
  • How will we find out it has changed?
  • Who in Defra will own this data?
  • Where will it be stored?
  • When will it be archived or deleted?

Structure

  • Can this information be structured as data?
  • How do we create that structure?
  • Will we need an 'other' field, at least to start with?
