This document provides specific, detailed evidence showing how the Defra Forms Designer meets Service Standard point 4 requirements, using concrete examples with dates, participant quotes, actions taken, and results.

Prototype site: https://dxt-prototype-682ae311ff10.herokuapp.com/
Design history index: https://dxt-prototype-682ae311ff10.herokuapp.com/design-history/index.html

Executive Summary

The Defra Forms Designer demonstrates comprehensive evidence of meeting Service Standard point 4 (Make the service simple to use) through systematic user research, iterative design improvements, and continuous validation with real users.

Key Achievements

Comprehensive User Research Programme

  • 13 rounds of user research conducted between May 2024 and April 2025
  • Over 50 participants including content designers, proxy users, and internal stakeholders across multiple Defra departments
  • 77 research iterations across 33 features, with an average of 2.3 iterations per feature
  • 27 features (82%) marked as "Tested" and validated with users

Complete Service Redesign

  • Reprioritised product backlog to address significant usability issues identified in initial evaluation (March 2024)
  • Transformed from component-driven, tool-heavy experience to task-driven, guided approach
  • Users consistently report the service as "intuitive", "clean", and "methodical step by step"

Continuous Improvement Based on User Feedback

  • Every major change documented with research findings and user quotes
  • Specific examples include: terminology improvements ("operator" to "Condition type"), flow improvements (question text before options), and feature enhancements (bulk option entry)
  • Issues identified in one research round are addressed and validated in subsequent rounds

Accessibility and Inclusive Design

  • WCAG 2.2 compliance focus with independent accessibility review by Defra accessibility team
  • Detailed screen reader support implemented (e.g., list reordering with clear announcements)
  • 361+ references to accessibility across the codebase
  • Training and support provided to all users regardless of digital skill level

Comprehensive Testing

  • Both online features and offline communications (confirmation emails) tested
  • Responsive design implemented for mobile, tablet, and desktop devices
  • All parts of the service tested with actual and potential users

Data-Driven Decision Making

  • AI compliance checker automatically validates forms against GDS standards
  • Research data systematically collected, documented, and used to inform design decisions
  • Feedback mechanisms in place for ongoing monitoring

Evidence Structure

This document provides detailed evidence using the Situation, Task, Action, Result (STAR) format, with:

  • Specific dates and research round references
  • Direct participant quotes
  • Concrete actions taken
  • Measurable results and outcomes
  • Links to design history documentation and prototype implementations

All evidence is verifiable through the design history documentation and live prototype, demonstrating a systematic, user-centred approach to making the service simple to use.
