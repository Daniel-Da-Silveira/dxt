There are a few ways to get feedback. Check document permissions and make sure everyone is comfortable with the tool you are using, like Mural.

Consider copying the content into a Word document that everyone can access.

If everyone involved is comfortable with Mural, you can ask SMEs to leave feedback on sticky notes.

Common mistakes in forms

Look out for these errors.

  • Asking for multiple types of contact information when you only need one
  • Asking for contact information, like a phone number, with no way to call
  • Asking for a title alongside name
  • Using the word please in hint text, or overly long hint text
  • Replicating phone call scripts and PDF forms without reviewing form structure and questions

Addressing feedback

Review the feedback and make appropriate changes to the form. It's a good idea to keep versions of the form in case you need to revert back.

If the SME has asked for something that's not possible or goes against the design principles or accessibility guidelines, provide the reason why you haven't made the change.

2i

Ask a colleague to review (or 2i) your work. You could set up a Slack or Teams channel specifically for form 2i-ing or follow whatever method works for you. See Reviewing and approving content for more information.

The 2i checklist is a good starting point.
