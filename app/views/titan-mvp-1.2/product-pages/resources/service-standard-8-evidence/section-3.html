3. Methods to iterate and improve design when there are time constraints

The team has established methods for making meaningful improvements even when time is limited, focusing on quick wins and prioritising changes that deliver the most value.

Example 1: Rapid Terminology Improvements

Situation:
Round 11 (March 2025) identified that the term "operator" was confusing users. This was a terminology issue that could be addressed quickly without major design changes.

Task:
Improve terminology to use plain English while maintaining functionality, within time constraints.

Action:

  • Renamed "operator" to "Condition type" (plain English)
  • Revealed the operator field that was previously hidden
  • Updated all related content and labels
  • Tested in Round 12 (March 2025) - just weeks later

Result:

  • Round 12 testing confirmed improved understanding
  • Quick change that addressed user confusion without major redesign
  • Demonstrated ability to make rapid improvements based on feedback

Example 2: Incremental Flow Improvements

Situation:
Round 11 found users were confused by the question creation flow requiring options before question text. A complete redesign wasn't feasible immediately, but the flow could be improved incrementally.

Task:
Improve the question creation flow to match user mental models while working within technical and time constraints.

Action:

  • Changed flow to allow question text first, then options
  • Kept all options on same page for better visibility
  • Implemented bulk entry for faster option creation
  • Tested in Round 12 (March 2025) - rapid turnaround

Result:

  • Round 12 testing: "No usability issues observed; setup was clear"
  • Users successfully completed question creation without confusion
  • Improved logical flow matches user mental model
  • Demonstrated iterative improvement within time constraints

Example 3: Quick Discoverability Fixes

Situation:
Round 11 found that "Filter pages by condition" was hidden in a dropdown and users missed it. This was a discoverability issue that could be addressed quickly.

Task:
Improve discoverability of condition filtering without major redesign.

Action:

  • Moved "Filter pages by condition" to left-hand pane
  • Made it visible by default rather than hidden in dropdown
  • Tested in Round 12 (March 2025)

Result:

  • Round 12 testing: "You can filter pages by condition. OK, I can see that would be useful."
  • Improved discoverability with minimal design changes
  • Quick win that addressed user need
