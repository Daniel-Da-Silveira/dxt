A pre-requisite to using the Defra Form Designer and working with the Defra Forms team is to confirm how you will measure the success of the forms you create.

Confirm what you will measure

Examples include:

  • reduction in failure demand rate (the need to contact users if the information they've provided was either missing or not detailed enough)
  • reduction of 'other touch points' (prevent or reduce the need for a user seek support from other channels when filling in a form)
  • reduction in processing times

If you're replacing an existing paper-based form with a new digital form, you must report on how you'll reduce failure demand rate.

A member of the Defra Forms team will contact you to agree:

  • the success measures that will work best for your form
  • the monitoring period, for example, 3, 6 or 12 months

The Defra Forms team will keep a copy of the agreed success measures.

Data comparison

You'll need to collect data before and after you make your form live.

Before your form is live

For your chosen success measures, you must either:

  • have data available
  • start to capture data to confirm whether the introduction of a digital form has had the desired outcome

You will be responsible for measuring and keeping a record of the performance of your form. We recommend having at least 3 months of data.

This is an important step as we need historical data to prove the form has been successful for your specific measures.

After your form is live

You will need to:

  • capture progress and stats relating to your success measures
  • report back to the Defra Forms team after your agreed monitoring period
