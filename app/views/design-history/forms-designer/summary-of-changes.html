Summary

The redesign replaces a component-driven, tool-heavy experience with a task-driven, guided approach that dramatically reduces complexity and cognitive load.

To see how we've improved the forms designer in-depth, feature by feature, see our feature level design histories.

Through 13 rounds of user research conducted between May 2024 and April 2025, involving over 50 participants including content designers, proxy users, and internal stakeholders across Defra departments, we identified and addressed several key areas for improvement in the Forms Designer. Here's how we transformed the experience:

  • From component-led to intent-led: Users begin by declaring what kind of page they need (Question vs Guidance), rather than hunting through a dense catalogue of components
  • Linear, guided editing: Designers move through steps with clear progression, replacing the modal-heavy approach of the current builder
  • Multi-question pages: Support for adding multiple questions within a single page, with intuitive reordering
  • Consolidated page management: All page-level actions consolidated into a single Page overview screen
  • Contextual choices: Options are revealed progressively based on user intent, reducing cognitive load
